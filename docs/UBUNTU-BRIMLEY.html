Common Ground Initiative · Toronto

Unlocking land for permanent affordable housing.

A structure that turns stalled, privately-held land into homes — the landowner keeps most of their value and stays in the upside, a non-profit owns and operates the building, and 30% of homes are permanently affordable. This project: Brimley Ward 23, 30 homes in Scarborough, Toronto. Every physical and market input is grounded in Arterial.

The opportunity

Land sits. Mandate waits. We make the deal move.

Across Canada, private landowners hold entitled or near-entitled sites they'd like to build but can't — capital is too expensive. Non-profits have the mission and the operating capacity, but not the equity to put land underneath the housing.

Ubuntu Land Trust is the connector. We bring the landowner, a non-profit operator, and the federal capital stack into a single deal — project by project. We don't bank land; we structure, underwrite, and coordinate. The building stays with the non-profit; the land transfers to them at Year 20.
