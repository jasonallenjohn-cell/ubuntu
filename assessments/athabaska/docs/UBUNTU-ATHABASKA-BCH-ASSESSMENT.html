1 · Executive position

Athabaska is a strong BCH candidate on land control and entitlement density. The drawings show a 43-storey, 516-unit residential development at 18–28 Athabaska Ave with only ~1,292 sf of non-residential use — so the land value is driven by the residential GFA and the 516-unit entitlement, not the commercial. Landowner inclusion works if the owner accepts a protected first draw plus deferred land equity rather than a full cash sale. Anchor land value to a $33.6M–$41.0M working range (appraisal-set); do not carry a speculative condo-land number without a third-party AACI appraisal.

Residential GPR ~$16.05M, less 3% vacancy (~$481K), plus commercial (~$47K) → EGI ~$15.61M. At a 1.25× DSCR sizing target, ~20% of NOI remains distributable after debt. The 60/40 split applies to net distributable income after debt — landowner 60% ≈ $1.16M–$1.27M/yr, BCH/NFP 40% ≈ $774K–$850K/yr — before reserves, taxes, and lender cash traps.

5 · Debt capacity reality check

At a 1.25× DSCR target and a 4.25% / 50-year amortization proxy, base NOI supports annual debt service of ~$8.12M and implied senior debt of ~$168M (sensitized $160M–$176M). This means the land draw must be staged and partly deferred: the landowner cannot be paid full market value in cash on day one without weakening the affordability model.

7 · Proposed BCH land draw structure

Land value converts into a 60% first draw paid to the owner plus 40% deferred land equity (VTB), with the owner's draw protected at a minimum of $17.0M. The Day-1 draw available across the appraisal range:

The owner's draw never falls below $17.0M — the 60% draw only reaches that floor near $28.3M land value. Delivery is staged: a refundable control deposit, a credited diligence/SPA draw, then the balance of the 60% draw at construction-financing close; the 40% deferred equity carries to the Year-20 transfer.

8 · Conditions before binding
  • Independent AACI appraisal confirming land value and whether entitled status supports the selected buildable-sf rate.
  • Architect's certificate / planning confirmation for buildable GFA.
  • Quantity-surveyor budget validating hard, soft, contingency, and financing carry.
  • BCH confirmation that the landowner draw, NFP ownership, and structure are eligible uses.
  • CMHC MLI Select term sheet confirming rate, amortization, DSCR floor, affordability tier, and security.
  • Legal/tax review of the land lease, beneficial ownership, Year-20 transfer, HST, and capital gains.
9 · Recommendation

Proceed to diligence, not final commitment. The unit count is large enough to matter for BCH, 30% affordability models cleanly, and landowner inclusion is achievable on a deferred/equity-style draw. Main risks: high-rise construction cost may exceed what affordable/rental debt supports; current land could be worth more as a speculative condo entitlement than the BCH model can responsibly carry; and approval status / development charges still need confirmation.

Reconcile before external use. This assessment's operating figures (blended rent ~$2,592, NOI ~$10.15M, ~$168M senior debt at 1.25× DSCR) come from the earlier directional model and differ from the refined Common Ground pro forma in this package (blended rent ~$2,397 with 30% at 80% MMR, NOI ~$9.03M, $164M CMHC mortgage at 1.14× DSCR, $328M total cost). The land-draw structure above is consistent across both. Pick one operating model as canonical before this goes to a lender or landowner.
