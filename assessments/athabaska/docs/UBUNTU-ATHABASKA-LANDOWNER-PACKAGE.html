03The Common Ground Initiative
03 — The model

A four-step
land-lease
model.

Ubuntu LTD is the connector — not a fund and not a landbank. We bring the landowner, a non-profit operator, and the federal capital stack into a single deal structure, project by project. The buildings stay with the non-profit from day one; the land transfers to them at Year 20.

  1. 01

    Land contribution

    You contribute the entitled Athabaska site at its fully-approved value of ~$100 per buildable square foot. You draw $60/SF as cash at closing and retain the remaining $40/SF as equity.

  2. 02

    Non-profit ownership

    A non-profit operator holds full ownership of the buildings and operational control from day one, keeping the homes mission-aligned and permanently affordable.

  3. 03

    Financial structure

    A 40% Build Canada Homes forgivable loan, a CMHC-insured (MLI Select) mortgage at 50%, and a 5% City of Toronto contribution fund construction. Your retained land equity is the final 5%.

  4. 04

    Year-20 land purchase

    At Year 20, twenty years of mortgage paydown together with the equity remaining in the deal cover a fair price for the land — a clean exit for you and a non-profit that owns both the building and the ground beneath it.

A market sale gives you a single cheque and ends your interest.
