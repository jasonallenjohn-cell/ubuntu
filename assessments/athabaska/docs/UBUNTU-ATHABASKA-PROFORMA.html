01The verdict
01 — The verdict

It works —
because of the
forgivable loan.

On affordable rents, the building is worth about $213M on a market basis — well under its $328M cost. The deal pencils only because the Build Canada Homes forgivable loan, the City contribution, and the discounted land equity together fund the $115M gap that rents alone can't finance.

Read in one line

The only debt is a $164M CMHC-insured mortgage, comfortably covered at 1.14× and 77% LTV. The $131.2M forgivable Build Canada Homes loan carries no debt service — that is what turns a structurally under-water affordable building into a financeable one.

06Who gets what

Three partners,
three returns.

Landowner

$24.6M + ~7%

$24.6M cash at closing, plus ~7% cash-on-cash on the $16.4M retained equity from the $1.13M annual cash flow after debt — then the Year-20 land purchase.

Non-profit operator

516 homes

Owns the buildings from day one with no equity outlay; acquires the land fee-simple at Year 20 via mortgage paydown plus remaining equity.

Governments

122%

Program-level fiscal return by Year 20 via construction, payroll and corporate tax — a self-funding mechanism, not a net subsidy.

Residents

Permanent
affordability

516 deeply and mid-affordable homes, secured permanently once the non-profit owns land and building outright.

The forgivable Build Canada Homes loan and the City contribution seek no cash return — their "return" is the housing and the fiscal multiplier. That is what leaves the thin post-debt cash flow available to the landowner's at-risk equity.

07Sensitivity
07 — What breaks it

Rents and
the rate.

DSCR is the binding constraint. With 30% of units held at 80% of median market rent, the project needs an MMR of about $2,500+ to clear the 1.10× MLI Select floor at the base 4.25% rate. Below that — or a rate above ~4.5% — coverage slips under the floor.
