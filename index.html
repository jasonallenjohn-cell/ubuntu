Common Ground Initiative · Canada

Unlocking land for permanent affordable housing.

A structure that turns stalled, privately-held land into homes — the landowner keeps most of their value and stays in the upside, a non-profit owns and operates the building, and 30% of homes are permanently affordable. Our first project: 28 Athabaska Avenue, 516 homes in North York, Toronto.

The opportunity

Land sits. Mandate waits. We make the deal move.

Across Canada, private landowners hold thousands of entitled sites they'd like to build but can't — capital is too expensive. Non-profits have the mission and the operating capacity, but not the equity to put land underneath the housing.

Ubuntu Land Trust is the connector. We bring the landowner, a non-profit operator, and the federal capital stack into a single deal — project by project. We don't bank land; we structure, underwrite, and coordinate. The buildings stay with the non-profit; the land transfers to them at Year 20.

The model

One site. Everyone moves forward.

Four parties, lined up around the same building, each better off.

The landowner

Keeps the value, skips the risk

Contributes the land on a long-term lease — no cash, no construction risk. Takes a large Day-1 cash draw and a 60% share of cash flow.

~$105.9M20-yr return vs. ~$39M selling
The non-profit operator

Owns the building from Day 1

No capital down. 100% ownership of the building, 40% of cash flow, and buys the land outright at Year 20.

~$218.7Mincl. ~$187M net equity at Year 20
Governments

Fund it; earn it back

40% Build Canada Homes forgivable, 50% CMHC-insured, 5% municipal. A fiscal return through construction, payroll and tax.

Self-fundingnot a net subsidy
Residents

Homes they can afford

30% of units held at 80% of median market rent, secured permanently once the non-profit owns land and building.

155 homesat 80% MMR, permanent
How it flows

Twenty years, three moves.

Day 1 · Close

Structure & build

  • Landowner leases the land in; draws $24.6M cash.
  • BCH + CMHC + City fund the $328M build.
  • Non-profit takes 100% ownership of the building.
Years 1–20 · Operate

Run & share

  • 516 homes operate; 155 held at 80% of MMR.
  • One $164M CMHC mortgage, covered 1.14×.
  • Net cash flow split 60% landowner / 40% non-profit.
Year 20 · Hand-off

Permanent & owned

  • Non-profit buys the land; landowner's clean exit.
  • Non-profit owns land + building (~$187M equity).
  • Affordability locked in for the long term.
